Skin Condition Guide

Learn about different skin conditions, their symptoms, and treatment options

Vascular Lesions

Low Risk

Abnormalities of blood vessels that appear on or under the skin.

  • Red or purple discoloration
  • May be raised or flat
  • Can appear anywhere on the body
  • May cause discomfort or bleeding

Treatment Options

Laser therapy, sclerotherapy, or surgical removal depending on size and location.

Benign Keratosis-like Lesions

Low Risk

Non-cancerous growths that commonly appear with age.

  • Light brown to black in color
  • Waxy, scaly appearance
  • Looks "stuck on" the skin
  • Can be slightly raised

Treatment Options

Usually no treatment needed. Can be removed for cosmetic reasons using cryotherapy or curettage.

Melanocytic Nevi

Low Risk

Common moles that develop from melanocytes (pigment-producing cells).

  • Brown or black in color
  • Round or oval shape
  • Usually smaller than 6mm
  • Uniform in color and shape

Treatment Options

Regular monitoring recommended. Surgical removal if changes occur or for cosmetic reasons.

Dermatofibroma

Low Risk

A common, harmless skin growth that most often appears on the legs.

  • Round, raised growth
  • Brown to pink in color
  • Firm to the touch
  • Usually small (3-10mm)

Treatment Options

Usually no treatment needed. Can be surgically removed if desired.

Melanoma

High Risk

A serious form of skin cancer that begins in cells known as melanocytes.

  • Asymmetrical moles or marks
  • Irregular borders
  • Varying colors within the same mole
  • Diameter larger than 6mm
  • Evolution or changes in size/shape

Treatment Options

Early detection is crucial. Treatment may include surgery, immunotherapy, or targeted therapy.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

High Risk

The most common type of skin cancer, developing in the basal cells.

  • Pearly, waxy bump
  • Flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion
  • Bleeding or scabbing sore
  • Most common in sun-exposed areas

Treatment Options

Surgical removal, radiation therapy, or topical medications depending on size and location.

Actinic Keratoses

Medium Risk

A pre-cancerous condition characterized by rough, scaly patches on sun-exposed areas.

  • Rough, dry, or scaly patches
  • Pink or red coloration
  • Itching or burning
  • Most common on face, lips, ears

Treatment Options

Treatments include topical medications, cryotherapy, or photodynamic therapy.
